UniFucGrasp: A Human Hand-Inspired Unified Functional Grasp Annotation Strategy and Dataset for Diverse Dexterous Hands

Hunan University, Changsha, China
* denotes equal contribution
† denotes the corresponding author
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Dexterous grasp datasets play a vital role in advancing embodied intelligence. However, most existing datasets primarily focus on grasp stability, overlooking functional grasps necessary for downstream manipulation tasks. Although these datasets perform well in generating stable grasps, they generally lack support for dexterous hands executing human-like functional grasps such as opening bottle caps or grasping cup handles—capabilities crucial for enabling robots to autonomously complete complex tasks. Moreover, most current grasp datasets are based on high-degree-of-freedom Shadow Hands, which, despite their superior performance, suffer from large size, high cost, and complex control. Inspired by the underactuated mechanisms in the human hand, future research should shift focus to underactuated dexterous hands that offer a better balance of structure, control, and cost while possessing the potential for human-like functional manipulation. Currently, there is a lack of functional grasp datasets targeting underactuated dexterous hands. To address this gap, we propose UniFucGrasp, a universal functional grasp annotation strategy and dataset for multiple dexterous hands. Based on biomimetic principles, it maps natural human hand operations to different dexterous hands, enabling low-cost and efficient collection of diverse, high-quality functional grasp poses. Finally, we build the first multi-hand functional grasp dataset and provide a functional grasp synthesis model to demonstrate the effectiveness of the dataset and annotation strategy.
